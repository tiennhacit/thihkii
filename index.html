Local Foods To Go

1
Goi Cuon (spring rolls)

Goi Cuon are translucent spring rolls packed with greens, coriander and minced pork or shrimp.

$3.95

2
Banh mi (Saigon sandwich)

Banh mi is one of the most popular street foods in Vietnam. Warning, very spicy!

$7.95

3
Banh xeo (sizzling pancakes)

These enormous, cheap and filling Vietnamese pancakes contains shrimp, pork, bean sprouts and egg.

$3.95

4
Bun Cha (grilled meatballs)

Bun Cha is a Hanoi specialty. You’ll find bun cha at food stalls and street kitchens across the city.

$12.95

5
Com tam (Broken Rice)

Com Tam is a quick’n’easy street-stand favourite that is almost exclusive to Ho Chi Minh City. Made up of smaller pieces of rice, it's also known as Broken Rice, and was traditionally a leftover-style snack.

$12.95

6
Cao lau (noodle bowl)

Central Vietnam does it best. Among Hoi An's tasty specialities is Cao lau, a mouthwatering bowlful of thick rice-flour noodles, bean sprouts and pork-rind croutons in a light soup.

$12.95
